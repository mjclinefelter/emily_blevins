How can I help?

Physical

Maintain or enhance motor skills and physical stamina. Support for post-stroke rehabilitation.

Mental

Maintain or enhance cognitive abilities and creativity. Decreases pain perception, depression, anxiety, and agitation.

Emotional & Spiritual

Provides opportunities for emotional validation, and reminiscence. Learn and practice effective coping strategies. Identify and express emotions.

Social

Enhance social connections with peers and decrease feelings of isolation. Increase self-expression.

About Me

In college, I studied the art and science of helping others meet their needs and goals through music. I earned my Bachelor of Music Therapy from Drury University in 2014 and completed my clinical internship training at John Knox Village Hospice in Lee's Summit, MO.

I have always had a love for serving older adults, and I specialize in providing end-of-life care. After receiving my board certification, I created a new hospice music therapy program with Oxford Hospice, where I currently provide services part-time.

In my remaining clinical hours, I offer music therapy services to support older adults and caregivers with a variety of needs and abilities.

I learned at a young age that music is a powerful medium for transforming human experiences and helping people be their best selves when I learned to play my first instrument, the flute, in seventh grade band. Music was my way to build self-esteem, process difficult emotions, and create connections with other people.
