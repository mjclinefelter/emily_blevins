    Benefits

    Benefits of music therapy are wide ranging and very individualized. However, here are some examples of the positive outcomes of music therapy.

    Physical

    • Maintain or enhance motor skills
    • Maintain or improve physical stamina
    • Support for post-stroke rehabilitation
    • Maintain or enhance cognitive abilities
    • Decreased pain perception

    Emotional

    • Opportunity for emotional validation, and reminiscence
    • Decrease depression, anxiety, and agitation
    • Increased creativity
    • Emotional support
    • Learn and practice effective coping strategies
    • Identify and express emotions

    Social

    • Enhance social connections with peers
    • Decrease feelings of isolation
    • Increased self-expression

    About Me

    In college, I studied the art and science of helping others meet their needs and goals through music. I earned my Bachelor of Music Therapy from Drury University in 2014 and completed my clinical internship training at John Knox Village Hospice in Lee's Summit, MO.

    I have always had a love for serving older adults, and I specialize in providing end-of-life care. After receiving my board certification, I created a new hospice music therapy program with Oxford Hospice, where I currently provide services part-time.

    In my remaining clinical hours, I offer music therapy services to support older adults and caregivers with a variety of needs and abilities.
